Spinors & SU(2): The Mathematics of Quantum Spin

Tangent Half-Angle in Vector Addition

The tangent half-angle substitution appears naturally when composing rotations:

Tangent Half-Angle Identity:
\[t = \tan\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right) = \frac{\sin\theta}{1 + \cos\theta}\]

For rotation composition:

\[ \tan\left(\frac{\theta_1 + \theta_2}{2}\right) = \frac{\tan(\theta_1/2) + \tan(\theta_2/2)}{1 - \tan(\theta_1/2)\tan(\theta_2/2)} \]

SU(2) and Spinor Mechanism

SU(2) Group:
\[ SU(2) = \left\{ \begin{pmatrix} \alpha & -\bar{\beta} \\ \beta & \bar{\alpha} \end{pmatrix} : |\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1 \right\} \]

Spinor Representation

A spinor is a two-component complex vector:

\[ \xi = \begin{pmatrix} \xi_1 \\ \xi_2 \end{pmatrix} \]

Under SU(2) transformation:

\[ \xi \mapsto U\xi, \quad U \in SU(2) \]

Möbius Transformation Connection

The stereographic projection to Riemann sphere:

\[ z = \frac{\xi_1}{\xi_2} \mapsto \frac{az + b}{cz + d} \]
Complex Conjugate Pairing:
For spinor \(\xi = \begin{pmatrix} \xi_1 \\ \xi_2 \end{pmatrix}\), the conjugate spinor is:
\[ \eta = \begin{pmatrix} -\bar{\xi_2} \\ \bar{\xi_1} \end{pmatrix} \]

Rotation with Half-Angles

A rotation by angle \(\theta\) around axis \(\mathbf{n}\):

\[ U = \exp\left(-i\frac{\theta}{2} \mathbf{n} \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma}\right) = \cos\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)I - i\sin\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)\mathbf{n} \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma} \]

Eigenvalues: \(e^{\pm i\theta/2}\)

Pauli Matrices:
\[ \sigma_x = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \sigma_y = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \sigma_z = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \]

Key Insights

  • SU(2) provides a double cover of SO(3)
  • 360° rotation gives a minus sign: \(|\psi\rangle \mapsto -|\psi\rangle\)
  • Half-angles appear because spin-1/2 particles need 720° to return to initial state
  • Möbius transformations describe how spin directions change under rotation

Physical Interpretation

The two-component structure encodes quantum probability amplitudes:

\[ |\psi\rangle = \alpha|\uparrow\rangle + \beta|\downarrow\rangle, \quad |\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1 \]

Complex phases \(e^{\pm i\theta/2}\) generate interference patterns through Born's rule.
